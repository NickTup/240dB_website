Objective C, Swift, Arduino and iOS

Turning more on the software side of things, I am wanting to incorporate my skills with Swift and Objective C to make communication to an Arduino which will send sensor input to the app and play sounds, much like a drum trigger or similar music technology tool.

I am trying to incorporate a microcontroller AtMega328 with an iPad Air by Apple using a redspark cable to do all the serial communication.

[youtube=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8jpdXAPpR5A&w=640&h=320]

The microcontroller will give control to any iOS 8 device (iPhone, iPad brands) and it will receive signal coming from sensors (piezo transducers) in the analog inputs, the code converts these inputs as mere messages for triggering events and buttons that will happen in the device, like playing a sound, processing its pitch through a slow phase vocoder or record a sound and process it…

http://vimeo.com/118180151

My idea is to make a wearable device that can hook up to your phone, while you put the device to it, you can listen to it through the headphone jack. All the hardware is from your actual phone or tablet, my device is simply the UI which you wear.
